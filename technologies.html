Heat Pumps

Efficient thermal systems designed for modern infrastructure, combining performance, lower environmental impact and integration flexibility.

  • Low-impact refrigerant pathways including R290
  • Suitable for retrofit and infrastructure modernisation
  • Focused on long-term efficiency and thermal performance
  • Relevant for buildings, district systems and hybrid applications
Engineering focus

Heat pumps are positioned as part of broader thermal infrastructure logic, not as stand-alone equipment.

Thermal Energy Storage

Storage systems that help balance heat demand, improve efficiency and connect generation, storage and end-use more effectively.

  • Supports balancing of thermal loads
  • Improves flexibility of heat systems
  • Strengthens efficiency and operational resilience
  • Relevant for integrated infrastructure deployment
System role

Thermal storage acts as a bridge between generation assets, infrastructure systems and end-use demand.

Water Purification

Water treatment technologies focused on cleaner, reagent-free approaches with practical infrastructure relevance and environmental value.

  • Cleaner treatment pathways with lower process complexity
  • Designed for real utility and infrastructure environments
  • Supports resource efficiency and environmental performance
  • Relevant for pilot validation and applied deployment
Application focus

Positioned for environments where treatment systems need to combine operational simplicity, performance and long-term value.

Sustainable Infrastructure Systems

A broader systems category covering integration, infrastructure modernisation, energy-efficiency applications and resilient deployment models.

  • Connects heat, storage and environmental technologies
  • Supports infrastructure renewal and systems integration
  • Focused on applied efficiency and resilience outcomes
  • Relevant where long-term adaptability is required
Umbrella role

This category frames how multiple technologies work together in coherent infrastructure and deployment contexts.
