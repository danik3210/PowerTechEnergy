Heat Pumps

This technology area is positioned around efficient heating, engineering-led system design, lower-impact refrigerant pathways and infrastructure relevance.

Overview

Heat pumps are a key pathway for cleaner thermal systems, particularly where decarbonisation and resilience need to be pursued together.

Technology rationale

We approach heat pumps as part of broader thermal infrastructure logic rather than as stand-alone products.

Why R290 / low-impact refrigerant pathway

The low-impact refrigerant route matters where performance, future compliance and environmental value need to align.

Application context

Development status

02

Thermal Energy Storage

Thermal energy storage is positioned as an instrument for balancing heat demand, improving efficiency and linking generation, storage and end-use.

Overview

Storage supports more stable and flexible heat systems in both building-scale and infrastructure-scale applications.

Role in decarbonised heat systems

It helps shift and optimise heat loads while supporting lower-carbon thermal pathways.

Materials / storage concept

Concepts may vary by application, but the focus remains on operational value and integration potential.

Integration with heat infrastructure

Thermal storage should be understood as a bridge between generation assets, storage systems and end-use demands.

Strategic relevance

It enables more resilient and efficient heat management, especially in constrained or evolving infrastructure environments.

Development pathway

Technology validation, systems modelling, pilot logic and scalable deployment architecture.

Positioning

An element of energy-efficient systems and a core tool for balancing thermal loads.

Water Purification

Overview

Cleaner water treatment pathways matter where operational simplicity, environmental performance and long-term system value are important.

Reagent-free treatment approach

The emphasis is on treatment logic that can reduce complexity while improving environmental compatibility.

Infrastructure relevance

Designed for applications where treatment systems need to align with real utility and infrastructure environments.

Environmental value

Cleaner treatment models contribute to lower environmental burden and stronger resource efficiency outcomes.

Development and deployment context

Focused on practical pathways for piloting, validation and implementation in relevant system settings.

Core relevance

Environmental systems with practical infrastructure application, not abstract lab-only positioning.
